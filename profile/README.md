## What Global Software Companies Does

Global Software Companies publishes structured company profiles covering software development firms across dozens of countries. Each profile is built from multiple independent data sources and scored across six dimensions that predict delivery quality.

We are a research firm, not a review aggregator or lead-generation platform. Our revenue model does not depend on the companies we evaluate, and no company can pay to rank higher, appear more prominently, or receive favorable coverage.

The distinction matters. When a directory makes money from the companies it lists, the incentive is to rank paying customers higher. When a research firm makes money from the quality of its analysis, the incentive is to get the scoring right.

## How Global Software Companies Scores Companies

Every company in our database is evaluated across six dimensions, each scored independently:

### Technical Capability

We assess the breadth and depth of a company's technical stack, including programming languages, frameworks, cloud platforms, and infrastructure tools. This is drawn from verified project histories, public code contributions, technology partnerships, and team composition rather than self-reported claims.

### Delivery Track Record

Past performance is the strongest predictor of future delivery. We look at project completion history, client retention patterns, and verifiable case studies. Companies that can demonstrate repeated successful delivery across multiple clients score higher than those with a single showcase project.

### Client Reviews and Reputation

We incorporate review data from third-party platforms, but we do not depend on review volume alone. A company with fifteen thoughtful reviews and consistent themes carries more weight than one with hundreds of brief, undifferentiated ratings. We cross-reference review patterns against other signals to flag anomalies.

### Team Seniority and Stability

A company's team composition tells you more than its marketing. We analyze the ratio of senior to junior developers, average tenure, leadership experience, and hiring patterns. High turnover is a risk signal. Deep benches of experienced engineers are a quality signal.

### Pricing Transparency

Companies that publish clear pricing models, rate ranges, or engagement structures score higher on transparency. This dimension does not reward being cheap. It rewards being honest about what things cost, including companies that clearly communicate premium pricing for premium delivery.

### Cultural and Communication Fit

This dimension assesses factors like timezone overlap, language capabilities, communication infrastructure, and documented collaboration processes. We are transparent about the limitations here. Cultural fit is inherently difficult to measure systematically, and we document where our data is thinner rather than filling gaps with assumptions.

## Global Software Companies Methodology

Our methodology is built on a principle: no single data source is trustworthy on its own. Every company profile draws from multiple independent sources, including the company's own website and public filings, third-party review platforms, professional networks, industry databases, and direct outreach.

When sources disagree, we note the discrepancy and use the most recent verifiable record. When we cannot verify a claim, we say so rather than passing unverified information to buyers.

### Data Collection and Verification

Global Software Companies maintains a research pipeline that combines automated data collection, ML-assisted analysis, and human verification. Automation handles the volume. Human researchers handle the judgment. Every company profile is reviewed before publication.

Our database maps relationships between companies and the services they provide, the technologies they use, and the industries they serve. Where confidence in a mapping is low, we flag it for manual review rather than publishing it as fact.

### What We Publish and What We Protect

The six scoring dimensions are public. The exact weighting formula that combines them into a final score is not. This is a deliberate choice. Publishing the formula would allow companies to game the score by optimizing for the most heavily weighted dimensions rather than improving actual delivery quality.

We apply the same principle to our anti-gaming logic. If you know the detection rules, you can evade them. So we document the existence of these protections without publishing the details.

### Corrections and Updates

We update our data on a monthly cycle. If a company reports an error in their profile, we acknowledge the report within five business days and publish corrections once verified. We maintain historical snapshots of our data, which enables change-over-time analysis that most directories cannot offer because they only show current state.

## How Global Software Companies Differs From Review Directories

The software development services market has several well-known directories. Most of them share a common business model: companies pay for visibility, reviews drive rankings, and the platform monetizes through lead generation.

Global Software Companies takes a different approach.

### Independence Over Revenue Per Listing

Our scoring is independent. There are no paid boosts, sponsored positions, or premium placements. A 50-person firm in Colombia can outrank a 5,000-person firm in the US if the data supports it. Our rankings weight delivery quality signals over company size and marketing spend.

### Research Over Aggregation

Review directories aggregate what others say about a company. Global Software Companies conducts original analysis. We publish market concentration indices, quality-adjusted rate benchmarks, provider density rankings by region, and cross-cutting analyses that connect technology adoption patterns to delivery outcomes.

This means our content is citable by journalists, analysts, and procurement teams who need primary sources rather than aggregated opinions.

### Longitudinal Data Over Snapshots

Because we maintain historical snapshots of our entire database, we can track how companies change over time. Did a company's team stability score drop? Did their technology adoption shift? Are they expanding into new markets? This kind of temporal analysis is only possible when you archive your data systematically rather than overwriting it with each update cycle.

### Transparency About Limitations

We document what we cannot measure reliably. We dropped English proficiency from our Cultural and Communication Fit dimension because we had no reliable method to assess it across our dataset. Honesty about limitations builds more trust than false comprehensiveness.

## Who Uses Global Software Companies

Our research serves several audiences:

**Technology buyers and procurement teams** use our company profiles and scoring to build shortlists of development partners. Our structured data and consistent methodology make it possible to compare companies across regions and specializations on equal terms.

**Industry analysts and journalists** cite our market research and original data products. Because we publish primary analysis rather than aggregating third-party reviews, our findings carry the weight of original research.

**Software development companies themselves** use our profiles to understand how they compare against peers and where they can improve. Because our scoring dimensions are public, companies know exactly what we measure and can make targeted improvements.

## The Principles Behind Global Software Companies

We built Global Software Companies around a few convictions about how this market should work.

Everything we publish is written for the person spending the money, not the company taking it. If a finding is unflattering to the industry, we publish it anyway.

Our analysis is structured around verifiable data points and documented methodology. When we make a judgment call, we label it as such. Our Methodology page includes a Limitations and Honest Caveats section, and we direct readers there before they rely on any single score.

Independence is the foundation. The moment our revenue depends on the companies we evaluate, our analysis loses credibility. We have structured our business to avoid this conflict entirely.
